How this was made

Built for a 1st grader

Every choice — the colors, the words, the avatar, the microphone — was picked with a 6-year-old in mind.

💬

Words kids actually know

  • Short sentences and simple vocabulary — no big words.
  • Gus talks like a friend, not a computer. "Hey, what's your name?" beats "Please input your identifier."
  • Cheerful reactions like "You're the best!" because 1st graders love to be cheered on.
🎙️

Talking, not just typing

  • Most 1st graders can't type fast — so they can press a microphone and just talk.
  • The mic button pulses when it's listening so kids know it's their turn.
  • Words appear as they say them, so they feel the magic happen live.
🎨

Bright, playful, bold

  • Big buttons that are easy to tap — perfect for small fingers.
  • Emojis everywhere because even kids who can't read yet can understand a 🦉.
  • Animations and a bouncing avatar keep the experience feeling alive and fun, not boring.
🛟

Safe & simple

  • No accounts, no passwords, no data saved — kids just show up and play.
  • No way to get lost — only one button to press at a time.
  • Every answer leads somewhere good — even gibberish gets a fun animal match.
🎯

Why Gus matters to a Product Manager

  • Vision: Start with the customer — who are they, what would make them happy? (Here: kids who love animals and surprises.)
  • Design: Make it look and feel right for that customer. A 1st grader needs something different than an adult.
  • Engineering: Make it actually work — the mic, the matching, the animations, all the pieces behind the scenes.
  • Listen & improve: Watch real kids use it. Notice when they smile or get confused. Make it better next time.
